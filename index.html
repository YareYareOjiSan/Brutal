Ironically, one of the earliest major proponents of Brutalist architecture is now a popular choice for contemporary offices: architects like Richard Rogers, Ben Cramer, Richard Caro, Daniel Burstein, the late Gennaro Bettoni, Norman Foster, and others.
Brutalism is partly a legacy of the Iron Age, when brick and mortar were the building materials for monumental buildings in Rome and Greece. Brutalists and Modernists alike attacked the fabric of existing architecture.
More recently, in the 1960s and early 1970s, Brutalism seemed cool. Architects and artists were mixing concrete with brash colors, echoing elements of ancient civil architecture.
